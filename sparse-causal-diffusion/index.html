Causality in Video Diffusers is Separable from Denoising

Abstract

We introduce Separable Causal Diffusion (SCD), a novel architecture that decouples temporal reasoning from frame-wise denoising in video generation models. Our key insight is that causal reasoning across frames can be separated from the iterative denoising process, leading to significant efficiency gains without compromising quality.

SCD splits the video diffuser into two components: (1) a causal encoder that runs once per frame outside the denoising loop to produce temporal context, and (2) a lightweight frame-wise decoder that performs multiple denoising steps conditioned on this context. This separation enables ~2.4× speedup (11.1 FPS vs 4.7 FPS for baselines) while maintaining competitive quality metrics across multiple benchmarks including TECO-Minecraft, UCF-101, and RealEstate10K.

Method Overview

Our approach is based on the observation that early and middle layers in video diffusion models exhibit high feature similarity across denoising steps, suggesting redundant computation. We leverage this insight to separate the architecture into:

  • Causal Reasoning Encoder (Eφ): Processes temporal context once per frame
  • Frame-wise Diffusion Decoder (Dθ): Performs iterative denoising conditioned on temporal context

This design amortizes the cost of temporal reasoning across multiple denoising steps, achieving significant efficiency gains while maintaining generation quality.
