Turning Video Models into Generalist Robot Policies

VJAM  the Video–Jacobian–Action Model

Headline

Across embodiments, skills, and environments

A 14B video generative model paired with a faithful Jacobian-IDM tackles a wide range of manipulation challenges — from zero-shot pick-and-place on a real Panda arm to contact-rich 16-DoF in-hand cube re-orientation.

Abstract

Decoupled video planning, faithful inverse dynamics

Video generative models have emerged as a promising robotics backbone, capable of generating videos that depict the completion of complex tasks across embodiments and environments. Many current approaches finetune video models with action-labeled data, turning them into robot foundation models that jointly predict future observations and actions.

We study an alternative, underexplored route: leave the video planner as is, and train an embodiment-specific inverse dynamics model (IDM). This decoupling lets the planner remain embodiment-agnostic, lets video backbones be swapped without re-training the IDM, and lets the IDM be trained from autonomous self-play data.

Our policy combines an action-free video world model with a structured IDM based on the robot embodiment Jacobian. The result is a faithful video-to-action translator that is data-efficient and scales to high-dimensional action spaces. We call this policy the Video–Jacobian–Action Model (VJAM); it achieves strong zero-shot performance on a real Panda arm and 16-DoF Allegro-hand cube re-orientation, and the same video planner drives both bodies via different embodiment-specific IDMs.

Pipeline

A planner, a translator, a closed loop

A video model proposes a short visual plan; the Jacobian-IDM inverts each step into actions; the robot executes and the loop repeats. One primitive subsumes perception, planning, and control.

Main results · multi-embodiment

One video planner, two very different bodies

A single action-free video model, post-trained on a mixture of DROID and Allegro robot videos, generates plans for both a 7-DoF Panda arm and a 16-DoF Allegro hand — we then pair it with an embodiment-specific Jacobian-IDM per body. Both Panda manipulation and contact-rich in-hand reorientation are produced zero-shot.

Why this works · ablation

J-IDM scales with action dimensionality

A controlled study isolates what makes faithful translation possible: gains come from constraining actions to enter through a learned tangent map.

a. Rollouts of recovered actions for direct-IDM baselines and J-IDM as DoFs grow.

b. At a fixed data budget, the J-IDM gap over direct-IDMs widens with DoF.

c. At high DoF, J-IDM has a better data–accuracy trade-off across training-set sizes.
VJAM vs robot foundation models

Reasoning through occlusions

Faithful translation also preserves the planner's reasoning. We hide a target button behind a partial occluder — visible from only one camera view — and compare VJAM against a recent video–action baseline. VJAM produces a coherent visual plan that searches around the wall; the baseline does not.
